Frequently asked questions

What is a URL shortener?

A URL shortener is a tool that turns a long web address into a short, easy-to-share link. When someone opens the short link, they're instantly redirected to the original long URL. Read more about how short links work.

How do I shorten a URL for free?

Paste your long URL into the box at the top of this page and click Shorten. You'll get a short link you can copy and share right away — no signup needed.

Can I create a custom short link?

Yes. When shortening, enter your own custom code (the "back-half") — 4 to 30 letters, numbers or dashes — to make a branded, memorable link like theurlshortner.com/my-sale. See our guide on creating a custom short link.

Can I track how many people click my link?

Yes. Every short link counts its clicks. Create a free account to see all your links and their click counts in one dashboard. Learn how our URL shortener with click tracking works.

Do short links expire?

By default, no — your short links stay active and keep redirecting. You can optionally set an expiration (1 hour, 24 hours, 7 days or 30 days) when you create a link, after which it stops redirecting.

Can I password-protect a short link?

Yes. When shortening, turn on Password protect and set a password. Visitors must enter it before they're forwarded to your destination, and the destination stays hidden until they do.

Is TheURLShortner free to use?

Yes, TheURLShortner is completely free to shorten URLs, track clicks, protect links with a password and set expirations. See our URL shortener with no signup guide.
